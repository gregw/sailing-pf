An online database of elapsed times from Australian yacht racing, with statistical analysis and
graphical presentation. Developed by [Mort Bay Consulting Pty. Ltd.](https://mortbay.com)

The **HPF (Historical Performance Factor)** is the back-calculated time correction factor a boat
would have needed, averaged across its recorded racing history, to have been equal-time with a
hypothetical 1.000 reference boat. It is a historical performance measure — not a handicap system.

A Handicap is a type of Factor that includes policy decisions of how to age handicaps race to race
and possibly proprietary algorithms of providers to normalise, clip and/or exclude results. 

The HPF calculation uses elapsed times and measurement certificates (IRC, ORC, AMS) from
SailSys, TopYacht, ORC, AMS, and BWPS. Boats with known certificates serve as "standard candles"
that anchor the calculated values. Their IRC-equivalent handicap, expressed as a **Reference Factor (RF)**,
is derived by traversing an empirical conversion network between handicap systems and years.

Raw race certificate and result data is collected from publicly available sources and reproduced as
a matter of public record. HPF values and other analytical outputs are published under
[CC BY-SA 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/).

Note that **the Handicaps/Factors are ALWAYS WRONG**, because if they were correct then every race
would end in a tie. At best, they are an approximate model of a possible reality — a poor substitute
for One Design racing.

Also note that this project is in early development and almost certainly contains errors and omissions in both
the data and the analysis. Feedback, comments, requests, and contributions are welcome via
[GitHub](https://github.com/gregw/sailing-hpf).
